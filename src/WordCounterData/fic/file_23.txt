1003741 Hers Were the Hands... Hers were the hands of an artist, skilled and sensitive, able to do that which few other women could so. Hers were the hands of the craftsman, hands which had sculpted the finest forms, carving busts and bas-reliefs. <p> Now she would take any job : washing dishes or slinging stew, picking up trash for the Parks Department. <p> Any - any - honest job, working with sensitive, capable hands. <p> Hers were the hands of the unemployed, utterly useless appendages now. Nothing more than empty hands.... <p> Idle, unwanted hands. <p> Article copyright Tradeswomen, Inc. <p> Article copyright Tradeswomen, Inc. <p>
